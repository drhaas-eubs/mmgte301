1

The Venture Drop

10 min

Your facilitator will present the financial anatomy of a real startup — from first cheque to exit. A provocation about what it actually takes to build a venture.

Your task: Listen. Then ask yourself: at which stage did the venture nearly die, and what saved it?
2

Gallery Exploration

20 min

Open: Unit 4 Framework Gallery (Slibraries 13–16). Work in pairs.

Your task: Find the 3 frameworks most useful for someone who has a validated idea and needs to turn it into a funded, scalable venture. Select from different Slibraries.
3

Framework Pitch

15 min
Your task: Name your 3 frameworks, state which Slibrary each comes from, and explain your combination in 90 seconds.
4

Case Deep Dive: Revolut

40 min

Revolut: Building a Fintech Unicorn at Speed

Analyse Revolut’s venture process from MVP (a prepaid card with no-fee currency exchange) to a £33 billion valuation and 35+ million customers.

Your task: Form teams of 4. Using at least two frameworks, produce a one-page visual argument mapping Revolut’s funding stages, unit economics, and growth strategy.
5

Investor Pitch

20 min
Your task: Present your analysis as if pitching to an investor. 90 seconds. What makes this venture fundable? What are the risks?
☕ Break — 10 minutes
6

Second Scenario

25 min

Two co-founders have built a working prototype of an AI-powered compliance tool for European SMEs. They have 15 paying beta customers at €200/month, a burn rate of €8,000/month, and €45,000 in savings. They need to decide: bootstrap or raise a seed round? What are the trade-offs?

Your task: Focus on Slibraries 14 and 15. Apply the Bootstrapping vs. VC Decision Tree and the Unit Economics Dashboard. What would you advise?
7

Reflection & Personal Slibrary

10 min

Individually. Answer three questions:

Question 1: What surprised you most about the financial realities of building a venture?

Question 2: If you were starting a venture tomorrow, which framework from today would you use first?

Question 3: Which framework do you not yet fully understand? Write a 200-word explanation.
