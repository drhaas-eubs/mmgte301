1

The Idea Drop

10 min

Your facilitator will present a product that seemed absurd when first proposed — an idea that every expert said would fail. A provocation about what “good ideas” actually look like before they succeed.

Your task: Listen. Then consider: what made this idea look bad to experts but obvious in hindsight? The gap between expert rejection and market acceptance is where this unit lives.
2

Gallery Exploration

20 min

Open: Unit 3 Framework Gallery (Slibraries 9–12). Work in pairs.

Your task: Find the 3 frameworks that best explain how ideas move from inception to market adoption. Select from different Slibraries.
3

Framework Pitch

15 min
Your task: Name your 3 frameworks, state which Slibrary each comes from, and explain your combination in 90 seconds.
4

Case Deep Dive: Airbnb

40 min

Airbnb: From Air Mattresses to a $75 Billion Platform

Using frameworks from the gallery, map Airbnb’s journey from idea to global platform. How did the idea cross the chasm?

Your task: Form teams of 4. Produce a one-page visual argument showing: (a) how Airbnb identified the job-to-be-done, (b) how it diffused through adopter categories, and (c) what its MVP looked like.
5

Pitch Presentations

20 min
Your task: Present your visual argument. 90 seconds. Focus on the frameworks, not the story.
☕ Break — 10 minutes
6

Second Scenario

25 min

A team of three university students in Barcelona has developed a prototype app that uses AI to match freelance chefs with households for private dining experiences. They have 200 beta users, a 4.8-star rating, and zero revenue. How do they validate and scale this idea?

Your task: Focus on Slibraries 11 and 12. Apply the Lean Canvas and Problem-Solution Fit frameworks. What experiment would you run first?
7

Reflection & Personal Slibrary

10 min

Individually. Answer three questions in writing:

Question 1: What is the difference between a good idea and a viable venture?

Question 2: Which framework from today would you use to test your own business idea?

Question 3: Which framework do you not yet fully understand? Write a 200-word explanation.
