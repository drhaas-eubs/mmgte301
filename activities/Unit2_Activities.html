1

The Dilemma Drop

10 min

Your facilitator will present a real company that did everything right — listened to customers, invested in R&D, followed best practices — and still failed. A paradox, not a lecture.

Your task: Listen. Then sit in silence for 2 minutes. The question is not “what did they do wrong?” but “why was doing everything right the wrong thing to do?”
2

Gallery Exploration

20 min

Open: Unit 2 Framework Gallery on your device.

The gallery contains 20 frameworks across 4 Slibraries (5–8). Work in pairs.

Your task: Find the 3 frameworks that best explain why well-managed companies fail. Click into each one — read the definition and source. Write down the framework name, Slibrary number, and one sentence explaining why you chose it.
3

Framework Pitch

15 min

3–4 pairs will present their selections to the group.

Your task: Name your 3 frameworks, state which Slibrary each comes from, and explain in one sentence why you chose that combination. You have 90 seconds.
4

Case Deep Dive: Blockbuster

40 min

Blockbuster vs. Netflix: The Five Laws in Action

You are an external consultant presenting to Blockbuster’s board in 2004. Netflix has 4 million subscribers and is growing. Blockbuster has 9,000 stores, $6 billion in revenue, and 84,000 employees. Using frameworks from the gallery, prepare a visual argument.

Your task: Form teams of 4. Using at least two frameworks from the gallery, produce a one-page visual argument. Your argument must identify which of the Five Laws trapped Blockbuster, and recommend a structural response.
Tip: The best arguments will connect the RPV Framework to the specific law that explains Blockbuster’s paralysis.
5

Board Presentations

20 min
Your task: Present your visual argument at the whiteboard. You have 90 seconds. Sketch, point, explain — then stop.
☕ Break — 10 minutes
6

Second Scenario

25 min

A Munich-based automotive supplier employs 12,000 people manufacturing precision fuel injection systems. Their engineering is world-class, their margins are 18%, and their top 5 clients account for 80% of revenue. All five clients have announced plans to phase out combustion engines by 2035. How should the board respond?

Your task: Return to the gallery. Focus on Slibraries 7 and 8. Identify the 2–3 frameworks most relevant to this scenario. How does the RPV Framework explain why this company cannot simply “pivot” to electric?
7

Reflection & Personal Slibrary

10 min

Individually. No discussion. Answer three questions in writing:

Question 1: Which of the Five Laws do you find most counterintuitive?

Question 2: Has your employer ever been trapped by one of these laws? Which one?

Question 3: Which framework do you not yet fully understand? Return to the gallery and write a 200-word explanation.
