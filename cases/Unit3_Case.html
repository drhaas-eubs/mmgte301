Case: Apple App Store — Gatekeeper or Platform?

DMA compliance and the EU vs Big Tech showdown · 2008–2026

Executive Summary

The App Store is the sharpest current test of where a platform ends and a gatekeeper begins. The EU's Digital Markets Act designated Apple a gatekeeper in 2023 and forced structural changes in 2024: alternative app stores, sideloading, alternative payment processors, and interoperability of core messaging. Apple's response — the Core Technology Fee, restrictions on scam prevention, deep warning screens — has drawn a second round of EU enforcement. The case forces a precise answer to: what is a reasonable platform governance rule, and what is an abuse of gatekeeper power?

Timeline

2008
App Store launches with 500 apps. Apple takes a 30% commission on all sales and in-app purchases.
2011
The first major developer complaints about the 30% cut. Apple does not change its rules.
2020
Epic Games sues Apple after attempting to bypass in-app payment. Injunction later partially granted; Apple required to allow developer links to external payment.
2022
EU Digital Markets Act adopted. Apple is an obvious gatekeeper target.
2023
Apple designated a gatekeeper under the DMA for iOS, App Store, and Safari.
Mar 2024
iOS 17.4 in the EU: sideloading, alternative marketplaces, alternative payment processors. Apple introduces a Core Technology Fee of €0.50 per install above 1M.
Jun 2024
The European Commission issues preliminary findings that Apple's anti-steering rules breach the DMA.
2025–26
Apple faces further EU enforcement; US DOJ antitrust case proceeds; developers continue to test the limits of the new rules.

Central Tension

Apple's defence is that the 30% cut, the App Store review, and the closed ecosystem are what make iOS safe, private, and lucrative for developers. The EU's position is that these rules extract monopoly rent and foreclose competition. Both are partly true. The case asks where the line is — and who should draw it.

Discussion Questions

  1. If you were on the Apple board, what specific concession would you make to the EU voluntarily to reduce the probability of further enforcement?
  2. If you were the European Commissioner for the Internal Market, what is the minimum test you would apply to decide that Apple is DMA-compliant?
  3. The Core Technology Fee of €0.50 per install looks small. Construct a scenario where it would devastate a developer's business.
  4. Apple argues that privacy and security are outcomes of its closed ecosystem. Are there platform designs that deliver the same privacy and security without the gatekeeping power?
