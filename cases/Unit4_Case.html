Case: Microsoft — Nadella's Growth-Mindset Reset

From Windows to Azure — the cloud-first pivot · 2014–2026

Executive Summary

Microsoft is the largest public-company turnaround in history. Between 2000 and 2014 the company missed search, missed mobile, and was widely regarded as a declining incumbent. Under Satya Nadella's leadership (Feb 2014 onwards) the company bet on cloud, embraced open source, acquired LinkedIn and GitHub, invested $13B in OpenAI, and reached a ~$4 trillion market capitalisation — briefly the most valuable company in the world. The case is studied because the most important input was not technology — it was culture.

Timeline

2000
Steve Ballmer becomes CEO. Windows obsession, mobile dismissed. "iPhone has no chance of getting significant market share." (2007)
2010–2013
Windows Phone fails. Nokia acquisition fails. Mobile share falls below 3%.
Feb 2014
Satya Nadella named CEO. Year-one message: "learn-it-all, not know-it-all." Culture reset begins.
2014
Office on iPad — unthinkable under Ballmer. Cross-platform software becomes default strategy.
2016
LinkedIn acquired ($26B). Azure grows faster than AWS. .NET goes open source.
2018
GitHub acquired ($7.5B). Microsoft becomes the largest contributor to open source on earth.
2023
$13B invested in OpenAI. Copilot integrated across every product.
2024–26
Microsoft briefly overtakes Apple as most valuable public company (~$4T). AI-first positioning solidifies.

Central Tension

Every technology input Nadella used was available to Ballmer: open source existed, cloud existed, mobile existed, AI research existed. What changed was the willingness to cannibalise the Windows franchise, to partner with former enemies (Linux, Apple, Salesforce), and to recruit for growth mindset. The case forces the question: can a business be turned around without first turning around the people?

Discussion Questions

  1. Nadella inherited exactly the same technology and market conditions as Ballmer. What specifically did he change first? What would BCG's 10-20-70 framework predict he would do first?
  2. LinkedIn, GitHub, and OpenAI were all acquired or partnered with rather than built in-house. Is that strength or weakness?
  3. In your own career, identify one fixed-mindset habit you currently have. What would it take to convert it to a growth-mindset habit?
  4. If Nadella retired tomorrow, what is the single most valuable thing he could hand to his successor? What could destroy his legacy fastest?
